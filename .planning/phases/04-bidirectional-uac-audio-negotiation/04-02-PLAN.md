<objective>
Validate on the physical FTX-1 that the runtime UAC negotiation, speaker reuse, and FIFO partitioning from plan 04-01 actually work end-to-end. This phase's four ROADMAP success criteria (continuous waterfall, clean TX tone, real negotiation success, no FIFO overrun across cycles) are explicitly hardware-verifiable and cannot be proven by code review alone — mirroring Phases 2 and 3's hardware-checkpoint pattern.

The highest-risk open items from 04-RESEARCH.md are: (1) whether the FTX-1 advertises an exact 48 kHz sample rate on mic and speaker (Open Question 1 — if not, all four D-02 candidates fail regardless of order), and (2) whether the 91/18/91 FIFO split (already at 100% of the 200-line ESP32-S3 budget) holds up once CP210x's bulk CAT traffic runs concurrently with bidirectional audio (Open Question 2 / D-07). Any FIFO adjustment made here MUST be re-verified arithmetically against the fixed <=200 ceiling before flashing — an over-budget config crashes the boot via HAL_ASSERT, not a soft error.

Purpose: Confirm AUDIO-01/02/03 behave correctly against real hardware, resolve Open Questions 1-2, and empirically tune the FIFO split if D-07 requires it.
Output: 04-02-SUMMARY.md recording pass/fail per success criterion, the actual negotiated mic format, TX tone quality assessment, and the final (possibly adjusted) FIFO split with its arithmetic verification.
</objective>
